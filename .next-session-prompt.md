## The 3 remaining failures and what they actually need

These are **not tuning problems** — every direct-tuning approach has been exhausted. Each needs a specific new engine feature. Pick ONE and build it; the user has explicitly approved engine work.

### 1. Long Synth Hall — `decay(1.43x)`
**Problem:** DV's tail-to-body RMS ratio is 43% above VV's. Shortening absolute decay (`kVvDecayTimeScale < 1`) triggers `farTail`/`tailSlope` fails because tail ends too early. Late bloom to sustain farTail keeps the ratio high (bloom IS tail energy).

**Fix (easiest path — try first):** Implement a **time-varying damping envelope** — a per-preset feature that ramps treble damping UP over time (starts at baseline, increases by e.g. 1.5x over 500-2000ms). This bends the decay curve steeper in the early-middle window without shortening total decay. Keeps farTail energy, reduces the tail-to-body ratio.

Location: Add a new stage in `DuskVerbEngine::process()` (wrapper-level) or in `FDNReverb` (inner, private per-preset). Wrapper is cleaner but inner has direct access to damping filters.

### 2. Small Chamber 2 — `buildup(mid_tail_vs_onset=-9.5dB)`
**Problem:** Factory decay is 1.15s, so the mid_tail metric window (1-3s post-onset) is past natural tail end. Any late bloom to inject mid-tail energy re-activates the 125Hz band that the tail notch suppresses → breaks `freqDecay(125)` again.

**Fix:** Implement a **band-filtered late bloom** — a variant of existing `lateBloom` that runs its boost signal through a HIGH-PASS FILTER before applying. The filter cutoff is per-preset (for SC2, 300Hz) so the boost adds mid/high energy only, leaving the notched 125Hz alone.

Location: Pre-filter in `DuskVerbEngine` bloom path, controlled by a new `lateBloomHighpassHz` field in `AlgorithmConfig`.

### 3. Small Drum Room — `spectral(6.5dB), specMean(3.3dB), decay(0.75x)`
**Problem:** DV FDN topology produces systematic spectral excess at 125-200Hz (+6dB), 800-1.25k (+5dB), 5-8k (+5dB), and deficit at 12-20k (-6dB). Peaking EQ at Q=1.41 only delivers ~30-50% of its nominal cut — even `-15dB` peaks reduce the metric by only 3-4 dB. Bloom is essential for decay (disabling it makes decay worse at 0.56x) but IS the source of some spectral variance.

**Fix (most flexible):** Replace the 12-band peaking EQ cascade with a **low-order FIR matching filter** that directly matches VV's spectral response for SDR. Render a short VV snare response at setup time, compute the FIR tap coefficients that would shape DV's spectrum to match, and apply. This bypasses the weak-gain peaking-filter problem.

**Alternative simpler fix:** Add **low/high shelving filters** to `SmallDrumRoomPreset`'s post-engine stage. Shelves give broad, predictable 6-12dB gain changes (unlike peaking filters). Trade one peaking-EQ band for a low shelf at 300Hz and another for a high shelf at 10kHz.
